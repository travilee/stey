LINKS

 in school:

Project Equal. Washburn University's Gay/Straight Alliance. This website is greatly outdated, so it would be better to just email Travis for current info. Meetings and social events are announced on the STEY "What's Happening" page currently.

Other Gay/Straight Alliances: The following high schools have GSAs at this time: Topeka High School, Topeka West High School, and Washburn Rural High School. STEY has information on how to get in touch with your high school's GSA if your school has one. Don't have one in your school? We can help you figure out how to start one! (Big hint: Go to the GLSEN website.)

  in the community:

Gay Topeka. Offers information on the community in Topeka. Provides a calendar of events for Northeast Kansas.

Kansas Unity & Pride Alliance. KUPA is the place to find out about Kansas Pride week coming to you each June! The mission of the Kansas Unity & Pride Alliance (KUPA) is to form a network of individuals, allies, and organizations committed to educating Kansans on the diversity of the gay, lesbian, bisexual, and transgender (GLBT) community, and to give a voice to secure our full civil rights throughout Kansas.

 spiritual:

Metropolitan Community Church. The world's most well-known church for the glbt community. Topeka's MCC is a great place to meet other folks. It is located at 2425 SE Indiana, with services every Sunday at 10 a.m. and 6 p.m. Call 785.271.8431 or email Pastor Paul at paulmcc@cox.net for more information.

 support:

Freedom Group. GLBT Alcoholics Anonymous meetings Fridays at 8 p.m. (closed topic) and Mondays at 7:30 p.m. (literature study) at St. David's Church, 17th & Gage. Contact: Marcus at 785.234.6131.

Living Colours. A social/educational group for gay/bi men. Meets twice a month, sponsored by the Topeka AIDS Project. Contact Jason at 785.232.3100. For men 18 and older.

Parents, Families and Friends of Lesbians and Gays (PFLAG). Confidential meetings for parents, families and friends of glbt folks. Need a place for your mom or dad to find support after you come out? PFLAG is very supportive. The Lawrence-Topeka chapter meets the first Sunday of each month at 2:30 p.m. Call for meeting location: Bonnie Cuevas at 785.841.2345.
